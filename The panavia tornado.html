The Panavia

The Panavia Tornado ia twin-engine, variable-sweep wing multirole combat aircraft

Length:16.72 m

Height:5.95 m

Wingspan:Fully spread (25° sweep):13.91 m

:Fully swept (67° sweep):8.60 m

Wing Area:26.6 m²

Empty Weight:14,100 Kg

Maximum Takeoff Weight:28,500 Kg

Engines:2 x Turbo-Union RB199-34R Mk 103

Thrust:Dry:43.8 kN (9,800 lbf) each

Performance

Service Ceiling:15,240 m (50,000 ft)

Armament

Hardpoints:9 (3 under fuserlage, 4 under wings, wingtip)

Maximum Ordanace Load:9,000 kg (19,841 lb)

Compatible Munitions:Air-to-Air Missiles:AIM-9 Sidewinder, Aim-132 ASRAAM

:Air-to-Surface Missiles:AGM-65 Maverick, Brimestone, Storm Shadow

Anti-Radiation Missiles:ALARM

Bombs:Paveway II/III/IV, GBU-24, BL755 cluster bombs, JP233 runway denial system

Nuclear Capability:B61 and WE.177 nuclear bombs

Avionics and Systems

Radar:IDS/ECR variants:Combined navigation/attack Doppler radar with terrain-folllowing capability

:ADV variants:AI.24 Foxhunter radar capable of tracking up to 20 targets at ranges up to 100 miles

Flight Controls:Hybrid fly-by-wire system with analouge quadruplex Command and Stability Augmentation System (CSAS) and digital Autopilot & Flight Director System (AFDS)

Navigation & Targeting:Tornado Advanced Radar Display Information System (TARDIS)

:Thermal Imaging and Laser Designator (TILD) pod

:Laser Range Finder and Marked Target Seekers (LRMTS)

:Tornado Infared Reconnaissance System (TIRRS)

:RAPTOR reconnaissance pod

Variants

IDS (Interdictor/Strike):Primary ground-attack variant

ECR (Eletronic Combat/Reconnaissance):Specialized in suppression of Enemy Air Defenses (SEAD)

ADV (Air Defence Variant):Long-range interceptor with extended fuserlage and advance radar

Retired:2019

The Panavia Tornado has been a cornerstone of European air power since its introduction in the late 1970s, serving in various conflicts and undergoing multiple upgrades to enhance its capabilities
